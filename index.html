Asteroid Escape

Engine: Unity3D
Language: C#
Asteroid Escape was my first Unity3D project. Developed in the second term of my first year at Uni it's was inspired by Starfox 64 primarily. The player would take control of a spaceship that's flying through an asteroid field and had to get as far as they could and eventually be destroyed by Asteroids.
The game features dynamically generated asteroids that spawn when a player reaches a certain distance forward. There was also plans to add power ups to the game when it's in more than an alpha stage. A link to a very stripped down game can be found here! I do have plans to start this project again now I have a more extensive knowledge of the technology. Watch this space for that!

Project Ropes

Engine: Unity3D
Language: C#
Ropes was a project taken up by my house and course mate Elliot Blackburn and I as a rapid prototype for one of our second year modules. It's a 2 player adventure puzzle game with a twist. The two players are linked together with a rope. This involves verbal communication between players in order to solve the puzzles under the contraints.
We looked into taking a rope mechanic from the Unity store and implementing it, however this proved unsuccessful and so we set out to develop our own rope mechanic, this, was not easy. We did however manage to complete our goal and learnt a lot from it. The blog for this project can be found here!

Hide and Seek Shooter

Engine: XNA
Language: C#
For the last piece of coursework in our first year programming module we were tasked with making a hide and seek type game. This was built in C# XNA and the idea was to hide from the enemies using the wall and then shoot then by returning into view, whilst you were behind the wall only certain types of enemy could find you and when you were outside of the wall all enemies within a certain radius would start to seek you out and end your game.

Fast Food Till

Language: VB.NET
IDE: Visual Studio
This was the first big piece of software I developed, it's a till system developed for my A2 computing project. Based off of real till I developed this till so that all the buttons were customizeable and had different functions. In order to do this all the menu buttons are generated dynamically and all their information was stored in text files, perhaps not the safest of methods but it was how we were taught at school. The system also held totals for hourly checks and a daily checks as well as a stock control system and a staff hours tracking system which produced a bar chart based on how much a member of staff had worked.
